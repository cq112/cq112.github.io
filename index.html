✦  Who I Am

About Me

I'm Tongyu Qiu, an MPH candidate in the Social and Behavioral Sciences Department (Implementation Science Track) at Yale School of Public Health, where I've achieved Honors in all coursework. Before Yale, I graduated Rank 1 in Applied Psychology at Hong Kong Baptist University with a 4.0 major GPA.

My research spans mental health across the lifespan — from depressive symptom networks in rural children to age discrimination's effects on older adults. I work at the intersection of clinical psychology, public health, and computational methods, using tools like Bayesian network analysis, latent growth mixture modeling, and large language models to uncover patterns in how psychological distress develops and can be treated.

I'm affiliated with labs at Harvard Medical School, Yale YSPH, and the Chinese Academy of Sciences, working on everything from psychodynamic psychotherapy to LLM-assisted systematic reviews.

✦ Core Motivation
I do research because I grew up seeing how invisible mental suffering could be — especially in children and the elderly who often lack a voice. Data is a way to amplify those experiences, to make the invisible legible, and to translate suffering into systems that can actually help.

Beyond the lab, I believe that healing and creativity are deeply interconnected. The same curiosity that drives my research also brings me joy in the everyday.
